

The Earth Calendar is a complete calendrical system by Danilo D'Antonio, Laboratorio Eudemonia, characterized by:

    exclusive symbolic nomenclature qualifying the days of the week: Moonday, Atomday, Waterday, Winday, Fireday, Earthday, Sunday; the years within a septennium: Year of the Moon, Year of the Atom, Year of the Water, Year of the Wind, Year of the Fire, Year of the Earth, Year of the Sun; and the septennia within an epta-septennium: Septennium of the Moon, Septennium of the Atom, Septennium of the Water, Septennium of the Wind, Septennium of the Fire, Septennium of the Earth, Septennium of the Sun.

    year zero: 1969 (marking the Moon landing and the birth of the Internet), synchronized with Epoch Time.

    long-term cycles: Septennia (seven years) for macro-temporal planning, and Epta-Septennia (seven septennia, 49 years) for ultra-generational visions.

    universal powers: moral qualities for the enhancement of Biological and Synthetic living beings.

    celebration of planetary events: Equinoxes, Solstices, mid-seasons.
